EXP — 01
Creative Training

Training the Creative Muscle

In a world overflowing with fast content and rapid creativity, I decided to discipline myself — with no boss, no supervisor — to train my brain to think outside the box every single day. The real challenge wasn't ideas. It was showing up.

The Method
Daily creative sessions using four formats: free writing, ad campaigns for intangible concepts, existential questions with creative answers, and personal analysis of The 48 Laws of Power.
Sample Copy
"Imagine someone borrowed a skill and neglected it until it became damaged and unusable. They would then have to compensate the library — and retain the skill because they had already ruined it."
The Insight
"Success isn't about writing something brilliant every day. It's about picking up the pen anyway."
EXP — 02
Brand Strategy

Visualising Personal Identity

I wanted to challenge the idea that business is only about sales and numbers — and prove that emotional marketing builds something money alone never can: trust and loyalty.

The Method
Built a full brand strategy for a skincare clinic: defined the persona (Caregiver & Savior), tone of voice (friendly, educational, empathetic), three detailed audience profiles, and a content mix built on emotion over promotion.
Three Personas
Boudour — working mom wanting fast solutions. Areej — needs real before/after proof. Dana — 20s TikTok girl chasing her Glow Up.
The Insight
"The most profitable thing a brand can do is make someone feel understood."
EXP — 03
Marketing Strategy

Bridging Creativity & Business

A real client, a real objective — increase appointments by 30% in 3 months. I learned quickly that creativity without strategy is just decoration.

The Method
Full social media strategy for Dr. Karim Nour: competitor SWOT analysis, audience segmentation, full buyer journey mapping, tone of voice, multi-channel content calendar across Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook.
The Gap Found
Competitors had high follower counts but low engagement and weak CTAs — no single dentist dominated social trends. That was the opening.
The Insight
"Good work is never just a skill. It's skill plus technical knowledge, working together."
EXP — 04
Business Proposal

Proposal with Creative Solutions

A strategic development proposal for Al-Humidan & Al-Khoaiter — a medical supplies company. The goal was to transform a stable business into a market pioneer, not just a steady one.

The Method
Internal restructuring plan · New sales and marketing department · Trained sales team · B2B expansion with government hospitals and NUPCO · High-margin product strategy · Cost reduction without sacrificing quality.
The Target
Grow annual sales from 60–70 million to 100 million within the first year.
The Insight
"Creativity and expertise aren't competitors. They're collaborators. The magic happens when both show up together."
EXP — 05
Creative Concepting

Deconstructing Fun

I believe every creative should understand all forms of content — including comedy. So I set myself the challenge of designing three distinct stand-up comedy show formats for real audiences.

The Method
Conceived three show formats — educational, interview, and analytical workshops — each designed for a different audience: general viewers and comedy industry insiders.
Why Comedy
Because a great copywriter understands how people laugh, what makes them lean in, and why certain ideas land and others don't. Fun is a craft.
The Insight
"You don't find your lane by standing still. You find it by trying every road."
